## 4. Business Recommendations

### Highest Activity vs. Revenue

**Tola** has the highest transaction count (**7 transactions**) and also leads in total revenue (**₦82,720**), so Tola is both the most active and highest-earning representative — a strong all-round performer.

However, **Abdul** has the **second highest transaction count (6)** but ranks **second lowest in total revenue (₦60,940)**

More notably, **Ese** generates ₦52,140 from just **3 transactions**, giving an average transaction value of approximately **₦17,380**, compared to Abdul's average of **₦10,157 per transaction**.

> ** Abdul's high activity with relatively lower revenue suggests a tendency to close **smaller, lower-value deals**. This may indicate that Abdul needs coaching on upselling, product bundling, or targeting higher-value clients to improve revenue efficiency.

---

### Trend Analysis: Should the Company Hire or Reduce in 2013?

**Recommendation:The company should not hire more staff but focus on optimising current team performance.**

**Reasoning:**
- Total quantity sold dropped from **476 (2010) → 380 (2011) → 368 (2012)** — a continuous decline of approximately **23%** over two years.
- Total revenue fell from **₦104,720 → ₦83,600 → ₦80,960**, showing the business is generating less revenue each year despite the same five representatives being active.
- Adding more staff during a declining revenue trend would increase overhead (salaries, commissions) without addressing the root cause of the decline.
- Instead, the company should **invest in performance reviews, sales training, and territory analysis** to identify why volume is declining before expanding headcount.
- If the trend reverses in 2013 Q1–Q2, a mid-year hiring decision would be more justified and financially sound.
